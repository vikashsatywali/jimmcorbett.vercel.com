Plan Your Days

An Ideal 3-Day Explorer Itinerary

Make the most of your Jim Corbett trip with our carefully curated suggestion for a 3-day full-immersion experience.

Day 1: Arrival & The Riverside Bliss

Arrive at your premium resort and loosen up. In the late afternoon, take a peaceful stroll down to the Kosi River. Enjoy riverside photography, maybe a quick picnic, and wrap up the evening with an authentic local Uttarakhand dinner by a crackling bonfire.

Highlights: Riverside Relaxation, Bonfire, Local Cuisine

Day 2: The Thrill of The Jungle

Wake up before dawn for an exhilarating early morning Jeep Safari into a core zone like Dhikala or Bijrani. Capture stunning wildlife photographs. After returning and resting, spend the afternoon on an adventurous guided Nature Walk or cycle through the eco-friendly village corridors.

Highlights: Jeep Safari, Wildlife Photography, Cycling

Day 3: Culture & Departure

Check out from your resort and head towards the glorious Garjiya Devi Temple. Seek blessings and spend the remainder of your morning shopping for local handicraft souvenirs in the market before embarking on a safe trip back home.

Highlights: Garjiya Temple, Souvenir Shopping
Common Queries

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about activities and things to do inside the park.

Are nature walks allowed inside the core Corbett zones?

For safety reasons, walking on foot is strictly prohibited inside the core national park zones. However, guided nature walks and bird-watching tours can be safely conducted on the outskirts and designated buffer zones accompanied by a certified naturalist.

Is it safe to camp at Jim Corbett?

Absolutely. The camping sites are highly secure, securely fenced (where needed), and constantly monitored by expert staff. You can safely enjoy your bonfire nights and nature escapades in these designated luxury camping zones.

What should I pack for these adventure activities?

It is highly recommended to pack comfortable earth-toned clothing, a good pair of walking or hiking shoes, binoculars for bird watching, a camera, and a hat. Do not forget to carry mosquito repellent during nature walks and camping.
